Avengers-Endgame (2019)

Directed by Anthony Russo & Joe Russo

After the devastating events of Infinity War, where Thanos snapped away half of all life in the universe, the remaining Avengers are left broken and scattered. Five years later, Scott Lang AntMan emerges from the quantum realm with a bold idea: time travel. The surviving heroes unite for one final mission to retrieve the Infinity Stones from the past and undo the damage that was done. But their journey through time forces them to confront their losses, make impossible sacrifices, and face Thanos one last time. In the epic conclusion to the Infinity Saga, the Avengers assemble for the fight of their lives, proving that whatever it takes, they will avenge the fallen.


Cast

  • Robert Downey Jr. as Tony Stark / Iron Man
  • Chris Evans as Steve Rogers / Captain America
  • Chris Hemsworth as Thor
  • Scarlett Johansson as Natasha Romanoff / Black Widow
  • Mark Ruffalo as Bruce Banner / Hulk
  • Jeremy Renner as Clint Barton / Hawkeye
  • Josh Brolin as Thanos
  • Paul Rudd as Scott Lang / Ant-Man
  • Brie Larson as Carol Danvers / Captain Marvel

Why do I like this movie

Avengers: Endgame is a cinematic event unlike anything before it. After 22 films and over a decade of storytelling, this film delivers emotional payoffs that feel earned, not forced. The final battle is pure spectacle, but the quiet moments Tony with his daughter, Thor's grief, Cap finally getting his dance are what make it unforgettable. It balances humor, heartbreak, and hope perfectly, giving each original Avenger a meaningful conclusion to their journey. When Cap picks up Mjolnir and when the portals open, I still get chills every single time. It's a love letter to fans and a fitting end to an era.
