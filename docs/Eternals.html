Eternals (2021)

Directed by Chloé Zhao

For over 7,000 years, the Eternals a race of immortal beings created by the celestial Arishem—have lived secretly on Earth, protecting humanity from the Deviants, their ancient predators. But after the events of Avengers: Endgame, the emergence of a new celestial threatens to destroy the planet. The Eternals, led by Sersi, must reunite after centuries apart to prevent an apocalypse they were never meant to stop. As they uncover the truth about their creation and their true purpose, they are forced to choose: follow the commands of their creator or save the world they've come to love.

Why do I like this movie

Eternals is one of the most ambitious and visually stunning films in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. Chloé Zhao brings her signature intimate storytelling to the superhero genre, focusing on character depth, philosophical questions, and breathtaking cinematography. It's a film about faith, loyalty, and what it truly means to love something. Unlike other Marvel movies, it takes its time to explore each character's perspective, making the conflicts feel genuine and emotionally complex. The scale is epic, but the heart is deeply human. It's bold, it's different, and it's beautiful.
